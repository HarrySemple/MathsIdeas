In the context of biological models, we can often split \(J\) into two components:
\begin{equation*} J = J_\text{adv}+J_\text{diff} \end{equation*}
Where
\begin{align*} J_\text{adv}\amp=u(x,t)v(x,t) \\ \text{i.e. particles @ }(x,t)\amp\text{ are moved along a vector }v\text{ due to some} \\ \text{advection property in the}\amp\text{ system - such as active trasport within a cell} \\ \amp \\ J_\text{diff}\amp=-D\nabla u(x,t) \\ \text{i.e. by Fick's Law, particles}\amp\text{ move down their concentration gradient} \end{align*}
Thus, the Advection-Diffusion Equation is given by:
\begin{equation} \frac{\partial u}{\partial t}=-\nabla\cdot (vu)+\nabla\cdot (D\nabla u) + f\tag{4.2.3} \end{equation}
